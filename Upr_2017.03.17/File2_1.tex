\textit{Excercise:}
Let $X_1,X_2,\dots,X_n$ be a sequence of independent and identically distributed random variables with 
$\mathrm{E}[X_i] = \mu$ and $\operatorname{Var}[X_i] = \sigma^2 < \infty$, and let
\[
S_n=\frac1n=\sum_{i=1}^n X_i
\]
denote their mean. Then as $n$ approaches infinity, the random variables
$\sqrt n(S_n-\mu)$ converge in distribution to a normal $N(0,\sigma^2)$.
